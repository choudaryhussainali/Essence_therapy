Still wondering?

Questions, answered

A few of the things people ask before their first session.

Can therapy help with burnout or feeling constantly overwhelmed?
Yes. Therapy offers space to explore the impact of ongoing stress and emotional exhaustion — reflecting on patterns, boundaries, and ways of responding that feel more sustainable over time.
I’m high-functioning but still struggling. Does therapy make sense for me?
Absolutely. You don’t need to be in crisis to benefit. Many people who appear to be coping well still carry stress, doubt, or exhaustion that therapy can help make sense of.
What if I’ve experienced trauma or something difficult in the past?
Past experiences, including trauma and PTSD-related concerns, can be explored gently and at a pace that feels manageable for you. There is no pressure to share more than you’re ready to.
Can therapy support anxiety or constant overthinking?
Yes. Together we can explore what fuels anxious or racing thoughts and develop awareness and strategies for responding to them in everyday life.
Can therapy support ADHD-related challenges like focus or overwhelm?
It can. We can build awareness of emotional and attention patterns and explore supportive ways of working with them that fit your life.
What if I’m not sure exactly what’s wrong, but something feels off?
That’s a perfectly valid reason to begin. You don’t need a clear label or problem to start — therapy is a space to explore what feels unclear and find your own understanding.
